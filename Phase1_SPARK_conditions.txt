G. HYPOTHESIS FORMATION
======================================================================

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H1: SPARKS REQUIRE HIGH FOUNTAIN CADENCE (output frequency, not input volume)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Sparks are more likely when the fountain fires at high frequency
(~20 fires/hr), regardless of sense input volume or diversity,
because rapid iteration of the cognitive cycle keeps the output
register in active, exploratory mode rather than settling into
groove-locked patterns."

Supporting data:
  - Sense input rate is IDENTICAL across all three periods (31–33/min).
  - Fire rate diverges 5.7x: A/B at ~20/hr, C at 3.7/hr.
  - When firing frequently (A,B), each fire has only ~90 sense events
    since the last fire — not enough to over-determine the output.
  - When firing slowly (C), each fire has ~533 accumulated sense events,
    enough for the sense register to dominate and produce formulaic
    SENSE_OBS output from the quiescent branch.

Testing: Increase the fountain fire rate artificially (e.g., lower
the readiness threshold from 0.70 to 0.60) and measure whether spark
density returns to A/B levels. If it does, cadence is causal.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H2: THE QUIESCENT BRANCH SUPPRESSES SPARKS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Sparks are less likely when the quiescent branch becomes a dominant
hot branch, because quiescent-branch fires produce short, stereotyped
SENSE_OBS content (clock-watching, hum-tracking) that occupies the
output register without triggering novel cross-domain mappings."

Supporting data:
  - Period A/B: 0% quiescent-branch fires, spark density 30–36%.
  - Period C: 20% quiescent-branch fires, spark density 6%.
  - Quiescent branch first appeared at 07:09:27 Apr 24 — after B,
    before C. Its emergence coincides exactly with the spark density
    collapse.
  - The quiescent-branch content ("The clock ticks...", "The distant
    hum...") shows mean word count of ~8 words vs ~19 in A/B.
  - These short fires dominate the corpus with SENSE_OBS template,
    producing the high hit-rate (72% in C vs 30–33% in A/B) because
    they are maximally predictable.

Testing: Monitor quiescent branch activation rate in real time.
When quiescent fires exceed 15% of fountain events over any 30-minute
window, treat that as a "groove-lock alert." If confirmed, test
whether injecting a novel sense event or koan into substrate disrupts
the quiescent cycle.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H3: KOAN-DOMINANT SUBSTRATE PROMOTES SPARKS; SYNERGIZED SUBSTRATE SUPPRESSES THEM
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Sparks are more likely when koan beliefs dominate the substrate
(open, unresolved questions) and less likely when synergized beliefs
(crystallized, resolved patterns) form a significant substrate
fraction, because synergized content provides ready-made resolved
patterns that the fountain samples without generating novel mappings."

Supporting data:
  - A and B: substrate is 46% koan — open question form, no
    resolved synthesis. Sparks at 30–36%.
  - C: koan drops to 33%; synergized rises from 0% to 18%
    (21 new synergized beliefs added at 05:27 Apr 24).
  - Synergized content is crystallized pattern output:
    "Understanding your paradoxical nature perfectly illuminates..."
    These provide the fountain with already-concluded observations to
    recombine, rather than open tensions to resolve.
  - The synergized addition coincides with the transition between
    B and C: same threshold as quiescent branch appearance (both
    emerged in the 05:27–07:09 window on Apr 24).

Testing: Run a session where synergized beliefs are temporarily
excluded from the substrate sampling pool. If spark density increases
toward A/B levels, synergized content is a suppressor. If density
stays low, the quiescent branch (H2) is the dominant cause.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUBSIDIARY OBSERVATION: CONTENT LENGTH AS SPARK PROXY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Mean word count per fountain fire:
  A: 18.2  B: 19.6  C: 10.1

Word count correlates with spark density (r direction: high words →
high sparks). Short fires are formulaic; longer fires carry more
internal structure, more space for novel mapping, more potential for
cross-domain leaps. If content length drops below ~12 words per fire
as a rolling average, that may be a reliable spark-poverty indicator
detectable in real time without running the full classifier.

Testing: Log word count per fire as an online metric. Define
"groove-lock warning" as rolling mean word count < 12 over 20 fires.
Measure false-positive and false-negative rate against spark
classifications from the existing corpus.
