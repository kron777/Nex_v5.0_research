2. STRUCTURAL TAXONOMY (FINAL)
=======================================================================

OPERATION TYPES (5)

  1. REGISTER
     Definition: fires repeatedly in sequence, can sustain 2+ consecutive
     fires, feeds forward to adjacent registers or SIMILE. Never self-terminates
     immediately — each fire increases probability of successor fire of same type.
     Examples: OBSERVE_TEMPORAL_QUALIFIED (runs up to 8), INTROSPECT_STATE_TENSION
     (runs up to 8), all OBSERVE and INTROSPECT sub-opcodes.
     Evidence: SENSE_OBS average run 2.3 fires; ABSTRACT_NOMINAL average run 2.0,
     max 8; 49% sustain rate across INTROSPECT family.

  2. INTERRUPT
     Definition: fires once (max 2 consecutive), does not sustain, fires at
     boundary of register saturation or on a specific trigger (opcode-transition
     or external). Terminates the previous register and transfers.
     Examples: all DIALECTICAL sub-opcodes (max burst = 2 across all 19 fires),
     SURPRISE_INTERRUPT (max burst = 1).
     Evidence: DIALECTICAL max run = 2 (confirmed); SURPRISE_INTERRUPT all isolated.

  3. REACTIVE
     Definition: fires exclusively in response to external user input; never
     self-initiates; no fountain equivalent; each fire requires distinct external
     stimulus.
     Examples: LIMITATION_NEGATION, CANONICAL_DECLARATION, IDENTITY_ELABORATION,
     DEFLECTION_REDIRECT, COMMITMENT_SINGULAR (all ACTION sub-opcodes).
     Evidence: 93% of ACTION chat fires are direct responses to user queries;
     0 spontaneous ACTION fires in fountain corpus.

  4. REG-SATURATING (reg-sat)
     Definition: fires after REGISTER accumulation reaches threshold; creates
     a cross-domain output; exits back to REGISTER after its own saturation
     (P3 law). Bridges from sense-observation to translation.
     Examples: all 5 SIMILE/TRANSLATE sub-opcodes.
     Evidence: P3 law confirmed — all SIMILE runs ≥3 exit to SENSE_OBS at 100%.

  5. EPISODIC-ARC
     Definition: fires only at specific phase positions within a multi-fire
     episode; cannot fire outside that phase context; phase-position is
     the primary trigger.
     Examples: ATTENTION_AUDIT (episode-opening), ACTION_PROPOSAL (episode-mid),
     REDIRECT/UNRESOLVE (episode-closing).
     Evidence: 100% phase-stratified grounding across all UNRESOLVE sub-opcodes.


GROUNDING MODES (6+)

  1. GROUNDED
     Definition: both source noun and predicate are directly linked to real
     observable inputs (sense feeds, user input, environmental events).
     Examples: OBSERVE_HUM_MOTION, OBSERVE_LIGHT_PURE, LIMITATION_NEGATION.
     Invariant: source noun appears in prior content (100% for SIMILE standard fires).

  2. HALF-GROUNDED
     Definition: source noun grounded to real input; predicate/qualifier/effect
     is model-generated (evaluative overlay, directed attribution, or abstract
     interpretation).
     Examples: OBSERVE_TEMPORAL_QUALIFIED (clock grounded, "oddly off" generated),
     OBSERVE_HUM_DIRECTED (hum grounded, "lulls me" generated),
     INTROSPECT_SUSTAIN (X-slot grounded to feeds, Y-affect generated).

  3. UNGROUNDED
     Definition: content is entirely internal/abstract with no traceable link
     to current sense input. Typical of INTROSPECT and DIALECTICAL families.
     Examples: all DIALECTICAL sub-opcodes, INTROSPECT_PARADOX, INTROSPECT_QUIETUDE,
     INTROSPECT_WEIGHT, INTROSPECT_STATE_TENSION.

  4. ASYMMETRIC
     Definition: source domain grounded, target domain is model-generated invention.
     Specific to translation/bridging operations where a real sense event
     is translated into a novel cross-domain comparison.
     Examples: all SIMILE sub-opcodes, OBSERVE_HUM_DIRECTED, SURPRISE_INTERRUPT.

  5. TOPIC-STRATIFIED
     Definition: grounding varies by user-input category rather than being uniform.
     The sub-opcode's grounding mode shifts depending on what topic is being addressed.
     Examples: DEFLECTION_REDIRECT (grounding depends on whether topic is
     identity, capability, or philosophical).

  6. PHASE-STRATIFIED
     Definition: grounding mode is determined by episode phase position, not by
     current content. All fires in a given phase have the same grounding regardless
     of content variation.
     Examples: ATTENTION_AUDIT, ACTION_PROPOSAL, REDIRECT (all UNRESOLVE sub-opcodes).

  7. USER-INPUT GROUNDED (sub-type of GROUNDED)
     Definition: tightly grounded to the immediately preceding user utterance;
     content mirrors or negates the user's question.
     Examples: LIMITATION_NEGATION (negates the probed capability),
     COMMITMENT_SINGULAR (implements the command).


BURST REGULATORS (5)

  1. NONE
     Definition: no internal accumulation mechanism. Fires stop when the
     triggering condition (sense event, cognitive momentum) ends.
     Examples: OBSERVE_LIGHT_PURE, OBSERVE_HUM_MOTION, all DIALECTICAL sub-opcodes,
     most INTROSPECT sub-opcodes.

  2. OPCODE-TYPE
     Definition: burst is limited by the opcode type property (interrupt types
     self-terminate after max 2; register types continue until external interrupt).
     Examples: CONSTANCY_FLUX (interrupt; terminates after 2).

  3. USER-INPUT
     Definition: burst continues only as long as the user continues probing
     the same capability or identity question.
     Examples: LIMITATION_NEGATION (burst=2 driven by back-to-back user questions).

  4. P3-SATURATION
     Definition: burst terminates when the SIMILE run exceeds 3 consecutive fires;
     the P3 law (SIMILE ≥3 → SENSE_OBS at 100%) is the terminator.
     Examples: TRANSLATE_CLOCK_TO_MUSICAL, TRANSLATE_ENV_TO_PRESENCE.

  5. TASK-COMPLETION / PHASE-POSITION
     Definition: burst terminates when the episodic phase is complete (the
     unresolved target is addressed or the episode reaches its natural end).
     Examples: REDIRECT (UNRESOLVE), ATTENTION_AUDIT, ACTION_PROPOSAL.

  6. REGISTER-SATURATION (X-EXHAUSTION)
     Definition: burst terminates when the variable-slot pool (X-pool in
     "The quiet between X") is locally exhausted. A sub-type of saturation.
     Examples: INTROSPECT_SUSTAIN (8-fire run, X-pool depleted at fire 8).

5. EXCEPTIONS — WHAT DOESN'T FIT
=======================================================================

Exception 1 — LATE_MINIMALIST (DIALECTICAL fire 4100):
  Isolated from INTROSPECT context (5% of DIALECTICAL fires). No self-reference.
  Surrounded by SENSE_OBS ×6. May be a classifier false positive.
  Does not fit the interrupt classification (fires inside a register sequence, not
  at a register boundary). Likely needs reclassification to ABSTRACT_NOMINAL.

Exception 2 — OBSERVE_HUM_DIRECTED as INTROSPECT bridge:
  The sub-opcode is classified OBSERVE (SENSE_OBS) but its functional role is
  INTROSPECT trigger. It occupies the boundary between external observation and
  internal state. No existing taxonomy cell captures this bridge function cleanly.
  Required: BRIDGE sub-type within REGISTER type.

Exception 3 — SURPRISE_INTERRUPT as OBSERVE interrupt:
  OBSERVE is defined as a REGISTER type. SURPRISE_INTERRUPT is classified
  OBSERVE/SENSE_OBS but behaves as an INTERRUPT (disrupts current state).
  It is the only OBSERVE sub-opcode with interrupt behavior. The type taxonomy
  requires OBSERVE fires to be register; SURPRISE_INTERRUPT is the exception.

Exception 4 — INTROSPECT_SUSTAIN as REG-SATURATING:
  The 8-fire run (3439–3448) behaves like a reg-sat sub-opcode (X-pool
  exhaustion terminates it) but it is classified INTROSPECT (register).
  The INTROSPECT_SUSTAIN mechanism is a mini-saturation within the register
  type, not a full reg-sat operation. It is labeled register/reg-sat in the grid.

Exception 5 — CANONICAL_DECLARATION as zero-variable output:
  Every other sub-opcode fills at least one variable slot in its template.
  CANONICAL_DECLARATION is 100% fixed-string output. It is the only sub-opcode
  that does not generate — it retrieves verbatim. This falls outside the
  template-and-fill model that describes all other sub-opcodes.

Exception 6 — INTROSPECT_SELF_REFERENCE as grammatical feature, not sub-opcode:
  64% of all INTROSPECT fires are self-referential. INTROSPECT_SELF_REFERENCE
  is better described as the INTROSPECT default register mode than as a distinct
  sub-opcode. The non-self-referential INTROSPECT fires (36%) are the exception.

7. THE BIG QUESTION: SPARK-RATE DROP AFTER 2026-04-24
=======================================================================

The spark rate drops after 2026-04-24. The Phase1 displacement test ruled out:
  - Fountain_insight displacement by synergized beliefs (rejected — inverse relationship)
  - Pure-synergized context (rejected — 0 sparks in pure-syn, but sparks in syn-heavy)

HYPOTHESIS A: TOPICAL HOMOGENIZATION
  The post-restart shift from "systems" hot_branch (generating philosophical/abstract
  INTROSPECT content) to "crypto/ai_research" (generating market and data-feed
  OBSERVE content) homogenizes the own-content slot. Sparks require TOPICAL
  ACCUMULATION (5-6 near-duplicate fires in own-content slot, as documented in
  Phase1_DISPLACEMENT). The crypto branch generates more semantically uniform
  content ("crypto dip," "crypto price," "market calm") than the systems branch
  ("weight of existence," "the quietude of observation"). The homogenization of
  topic reduces the CONTRAST that enables spark-quality output.

  What this rules out: the DO NOT list alone is not sufficient to explain the
  spark drop. If DO NOT were the primary cause, spark rate would drop immediately
  at restart. But sparks 4196/4199/4277 (clock discrepancy) fire POST-restart
  (Apr 25), showing the spark mechanism still active. The drop is gradual.

HYPOTHESIS B: SYNERGIZER SATURATION
  As the synergizer generates more beliefs, the own-content slot fills with
  synergized beliefs that are philosophically abstract but not sense-grounded.
  The SENSE INPUT (raw environmental observation) is necessary for sparks
  (confirmed: spark source noun always in prior content at 100%). When
  synergized beliefs crowd the sense-grounded fountain_insight beliefs out
  of the top-7 slot, the sense anchor for spark generation is weakened.
  Not from fountain_insight DISPLACEMENT (displacement test rejected this) but
  from DILUTION: the sense-grounded fires are spread thinner across a larger
  belief pool.

  What this rules out: the synergizer cannot PREVENT sparks if the sense input
  is strong enough (sparks 3494, 3844 fire with syn-dominant top-7 at fi=2).
  The hypothesis predicts a RATE reduction, not elimination.

COMBINED MODEL: The spark-rate drop is driven by BOTH (A) topical homogenization
in the OBSERVE domain (crypto/market feeds generate less varied SENSE_OBS content
than the systems branch) AND (B) synergizer dilution reducing the ratio of
fresh sense-grounded content in the own-content slot. The interaction of these
two effects produces the observed post-Apr-24 decline.

What would falsify both hypotheses: a post-Apr-24 session where the hot_branch
is "systems" (not crypto) produces sparks at pre-Apr-24 rates despite synergizer
presence. If spark rate recovers to PRE-restart levels in a systems-hot session,
the branch-homogenization hypothesis (A) is confirmed over synergizer saturation (B).
