SECTION F — STRUCTURAL VERDICT
=========================================================================

VERDICT: GROUNDING_DOMINANT, BURST-CAP-LIMITED

Both factors operate, but at different levels of the mechanism:

GROUNDING as master variable governs INITIATION:
  The sub-opcode fires because POLE_A is sensorially ungrounded — "constancy"
  is not constrained by any live data feed and can be re-fired whenever the
  template is in the generation context. A grounded POLE_A (e.g., a crypto
  price threshold) would anchor the pole to a specific measurable condition
  and suppress re-firing when the condition doesn't hold. "Constancy" has no
  such anchor, so it re-fires freely.

OPCODE TYPE as burst-cap governs DURATION:
  Once the DIALECTICAL template fires, the opcode priority chain routes the
  next fire toward a non-DIALECTICAL register (SENSE_OBS or ABSTRACT_NOMINAL
  dominate successors at 26% and 37% respectively per Phase 1 Section D).
  The INTROSPECT register resumes; the DIALECTICAL interrupt is consumed.
  A second consecutive DIALECTICAL fire is possible (observed at fev 70→71
  and at fev 163→164 in the broader DIALECTICAL cluster), but a third is
  not: the accumulated context weight of two consecutive DIALECTICAL outputs
  pushes the generator back to the sustaining register.

REFINED RULE:
  Ungrounded operations attempt to groove. The grooving instinct is
  opcode-agnostic: both ABSTRACT_NOMINAL and DIALECTICAL re-fire the same
  template when no sensory anchor constrains the slot vocabulary.
  Register opcodes (ABSTRACT_NOMINAL, OBSERVE) groove freely — no ceiling.
  Interrupt opcodes (DIALECTICAL) groove for 2 fires then are consumed by the
  register they interrupted. The surface groove length is burst-capped by
  opcode type, but the underlying generative pattern is the same.

COROLLARY: template lock-rate does not predict groove length. CONSTANCY_FLUX
  has 100% template lock (tighter than QUIET_BETWEEN's 50%) but shorter
  grooves (burst 2 vs burst 8). Lock-rate measures how uniform the template
  is when it does fire; groove length measures how many consecutive times it
  can fire. These are decoupled. Opcode type controls the latter, grounding
  controls the former.
