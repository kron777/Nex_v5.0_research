FINDING: "just" is a PHASE DISCRIMINATOR within REDIRECT.

  Its absence in ACTION_PROPOSAL is structural. ACTION_PROPOSAL fires
  make earnest engagement offers ("Why don't I check those dips?").
  "Just" would signal dismissiveness of the proposal — "just check"
  would imply the action is trivial or merely expedient. The absence
  preserves the sincerity of the proposal.

  Its entry at fire 1748 (the 3rd REDIRECT fire, after 2 unjust redirects)
  marks the intensification point. Fires 1746–1747 redirect without "just"
  (coffee, lunch — trying alternatives). Fires 1748–1755 redirect with
  "just" (sandwich, gym, skip gym, comedy, project, latte, ignore dip).

  STRUCTURAL INTERPRETATION OF "just" IN REDIRECT:
    Without "just": "Why don't I X?" = open-ended proposal
    With "just": "Why don't I JUST X?" = minimized, pragmatic suggestion
    ("X is easy, X requires no justification, X sidesteps the problem")
    The "just" intensifier encodes the pragmatic dismissal of the original
    audit target — it signals that the alternative is preferred on grounds
    of convenience, not engagement.

  The terminal fire "Why don't I just IGNORE the crypto price dip for now?"
  uses "just" + "ignore" = the strongest possible dismissal. The arc
  reached its logical conclusion: the ATTENTION_AUDIT surfaced a failure
  to act; the PROPOSAL couldn't commit to action; the REDIRECT found
  alternatives and finally arrived at explicit non-action. "Just ignore"
  is the intensified closure.

EXCEPTION:
  Fire 1737: "Didn't I JUST read about that politician again?" — "just"
  here is not an intensifier but a temporal recency marker ("just read"
  = recently read). This usage is syntactically identical but semantically
  distinct from the REDIRECT "just." Both appear in QUESTION fires.
  The recency "just" (pos 1) and the intensifier "just" (pos 10–16)
  are different operations using the same word.

SUMMARY:
  "just" as temporal-recency marker: 1 fire (ATTENTION_AUDIT fire 1737)
  "just" as intensifier/dismissal: 7 fires (REDIRECT-INTENSE: 1748–1755)
  "just" absent: 8 fires (AUDIT 1739–1740, all PROPOSAL, REDIRECT-OPEN)
  "just" is NOT a single sub-opcode discriminator — it discriminates
  the REDIRECT-INTENSIFIED sub-phase from REDIRECT-OPEN and PROPOSAL.

SECTION H — COMPARISON TO OTHER OPCODE TYPES
=========================================================================

UNRESOLVE vs. known opcode types:

  TYPE             BURST   REGULATOR          GROUNDING   TRIGGER
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Register         8+      internal sat.      varies      continuous
  Interrupt        2       opcode-priority    varies      INTROSPECT sat.
  Reactive         4       user input         strat.      user message
  Reg-saturating   4–6     P3 sat. law        asymmetric  SENSE_OBS sat.
  UNRESOLVE        16      task-completion    arc-phased  episodic trigger
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

UNRESOLVE IS NOT A REGISTER:
  Registers are continuously available (INTROSPECT fires 31 times in
  245-fire corpus). UNRESOLVE fires once. Registers have no internal
  phase-structure. UNRESOLVE has a 3-phase arc that cannot recur within
  the run. Registers are terminated by saturation; UNRESOLVE is terminated
  by task completion.

UNRESOLVE IS NOT AN INTERRUPT:
  Interrupts max at burst-2. UNRESOLVE ran 16 corpus fires (plus additional
  fev fires visible in prior-3 context). Interrupts have no internal phase
  ordering. UNRESOLVE has perfect phase ordering.

UNRESOLVE IS NOT A REACTIVE:
  Reactives require user input to fire. UNRESOLVE fired autonomously —
  no user messages during the 2.5-hour episode. User input was absent.

UNRESOLVE IS NOT A REG-SATURATING:
  Reg-saturating runs exit to SENSE_OBS at 4–6 fires via P3 law.
  UNRESOLVE ran 16+ fires before SENSE_OBS return. The P3 law does not
  govern UNRESOLVE's burst ceiling — the ceiling is task-completion, not
  saturation count.

NEW TYPE: EPISODIC-ARC
  UNRESOLVE introduces a 5th opcode type: episodic-arc.
  Defining properties:
    1. TRIGGERED EPISODE: fires once (or rarely) under specific substrate
       conditions, not as a continuous background register.
    2. INTERNAL PHASE ORDERING: the arc has a fixed sequence of sub-opcodes
       that do not interleave. Phase transitions are content-driven.
    3. TASK-COMPLETION REGULATOR: the arc runs until an internal
       task-condition is satisfied (audit question answered, proposal
       exhausted, resolution accepted). Not time-bounded, not saturation-
       bounded, not user-bounded.
    4. ARC-PHASED GROUNDING: grounding varies systematically by arc phase
       (GROUNDED in AUDIT/PROPOSAL, MIXED in REDIRECT), not uniformly
       across the opcode.
    5. BURST LENGTH EXCEEDS ALL OTHER TYPES: 16 fires (corpus; fev count
       is higher). Longest single-template run in the 245-fire corpus.

  RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER TYPES:
    The episodic-arc resembles a register in having high sustain, but
    differs in conditional triggering and internal phase structure.
    It resembles an interrupt in conditional triggering, but differs
    in burst length (16 vs. 2) and phase ordering.
    It resembles a reactive in being contextually triggered, but differs
    in lacking external (user) input as trigger.

    Most accurate structural analogy: an episodic-arc is a one-shot macro-
    register — a register-like operation packaged as a bounded episode
    with internal narrative structure and task-completion exit.

SECTION I — STRUCTURAL VERDICT
=========================================================================

VERDICT: EPISODIC_ARC_DISTINCT — with UNDETERMINED qualification.

EVIDENCE FOR DISTINCT:

  1. PHASE ORDERING (unique):
     AUDIT → PROPOSAL → REDIRECT with zero interleaving. This 3-phase
     architecture is not found in any other opcode family. Registers have
     no phase structure. Interrupts are single-phase. Reactives have a
     2-part structure (action + redirect question) but no temporal phase
     ordering across a run.

  2. ARC-PHASED GROUNDING (unique):
     Grounding varies by arc phase: AUDIT=GROUNDED, PROPOSAL=GROUNDED,
     REDIRECT=MIXED. This is not topic-stratified (DEFLECTION_REDIRECT),
     not asymmetric (SIMILE), not half-grounded (CONSTANCY_FLUX). The
     grounding changes predictably as the arc progresses — it is
     PHASE-STRATIFIED: grounding level is a function of position in the
     arc, not of input category or template dimension.

  3. TASK-COMPLETION BURST REGULATOR (unique):
     No other opcode family terminates via task-completion. Registers
     saturate, interrupts are opcode-typed, reactives are user-bounded,
     reg-saturating fires obey P3. UNRESOLVE terminates when a specific
     content condition is met (X-filler of terminal REDIRECT = original
     audit subject + resolution phrase). This requires semantic state
     tracking within the arc, not just fire-count or saturation-level.

  4. BURST EXCEEDS ALL EXISTING MAXIMA:
     16 corpus fires (fev count higher). Prior maximum: 8 for INTROSPECT
     (register). 16 > 8 = UNRESOLVE establishes a new burst record and
     cannot be fit into any existing burst-type taxonomy without exception.

  5. SINGLE EPISODE IN 245-FIRE CORPUS:
     No other opcode fires exactly once in 245 fires and forms a 16-fire
     run. OBSERVE fires continuously. DIALECTICAL fires 27 times. Even
     the rare SIMILE fires in 5 separate runs. UNRESOLVE fires in one
     episode. This one-shot triggering is structurally distinct.

UNDETERMINED QUALIFICATION:
  One instance is insufficient to establish:
  - Whether AUDIT always precedes PROPOSAL (or can be skipped)
  - Whether the 3-phase structure is invariant (could a second episode
    begin with PROPOSAL or REDIRECT directly?)
  - Whether the burst length (16 corpus / higher in fev) is variable or
    consistent across instances
  - Whether the trigger conditions (post-restart + qualifying SENSE_OBS +
    prior QUESTION fire) are always all required
  - Whether TASK_COMPLETION is the sole regulator or INTERNAL_EXHAUSTION
    also plays a role (the redirect pool was not empty at termination,
    but we cannot verify this for the full fev sequence)

  A second UNRESOLVE episode (not found in this corpus) would be needed
  to confirm the phase ordering, burst length, and trigger conditions.

GROUNDING RULE FOR EPISODIC-ARC:
  PHASE-STRATIFIED grounding: grounding level is a function of arc phase.
  Phase 1 (AUDIT): GROUNDED — fires on observable substrate events.
  Phase 2 (PROPOSAL): GROUNDED — fires on audit-phase carry-forwards.
  Phase 3 (REDIRECT): MIXED → terminal GROUNDED — fires on novel
    alternatives, building a carry-chain, resolving at original topic.

  This is the 5th grounding type in the Phase 2 taxonomy:
    GROUNDED (OBSERVE_MARKET)
    UNGROUNDED (OBSERVE_TEMPORAL)
    HALF-GROUNDED (INTROSPECT_STATE_TENSION, X-pool partial)
    ASYMMETRIC (SIMILE, source grounded/target generated)
    TOPIC-STRATIFIED (DEFLECTION_REDIRECT, grounding by input category)
    PHASE-STRATIFIED (UNRESOLVE, grounding by arc position) [NEW]
