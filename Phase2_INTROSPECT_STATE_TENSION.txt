  4. NO NEGATIVE VALENCE: the lock never fires "oddly heavy", "oddly
     cold", "oddly unsettling" — only positive-surprise Y values.
     This contrasts with STATE_ECHO fires (3844: "oddly heavy") which
     do use negative Y. The lock template is not just "oddly [ANY_ADJ]"
     — it is specifically "oddly [POSITIVE_ADJ]".

Y-pool appears to draw from a fixed lexical set:
  {familiar, reassuring, comfortable, comforting, stimulating,
   invigorating, refreshing, warm, soothing, invigorating}
This set maps closely to POSITIVE AFFECT vocabulary in the sense stream
metadata, not to any content in the immediately prior fires.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
D3. Lock mechanism: what drives 8 consecutive fires from one template?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Three reinforcing factors:

Factor 1 — CONTEXT SATURATION
By fire 2 (3441), the prior context already contains 2 copies of the
lock template. By fire 4 (3443), the prior context contains 3 copies.
The fountain generator draws from recent beliefs as context for the
next generation. Once 2+ prior beliefs use the same template, the
template surface becomes the dominant pattern in the context window.
Self-reinforcement compounds: each new lock fire makes it more likely
the next fire will also be a lock fire.

Factor 2 — CONTINUOUS X-SUPPLY
The sense streams continuously produce events (crypto.coingecko JSON
every ~2 min, agi.arxiv periodically). Each event provides a fresh
X-noun (market updates, crypto updates, scientific papers). As long as
new event-type labels are available, the X-slot can be filled without
repeating. This prevents the lock from stalling due to X exhaustion
— by the time X-synonyms cycle (tech news → tech updates → tech
headlines), 8 fires have been produced.

Factor 3 — Y-POOL AVAILABILITY
The Y-slot can always be filled from a closed positive-affect vocabulary
pool. There is no Y-exhaustion: even after 8 fires, 3 of the 7 pool
members have been used only once. The Y-pool is large enough relative
to run length that the template never stalls for lack of a Y value.

Combined: the template locks when (a) enough prior context uses the
template to dominate the generation context, (b) new X-nouns remain
available from sense streams, and (c) the Y-pool is not exhausted.
All three conditions hold for at least 8 consecutive fires.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
D4. Lock release: why does the lock end after 8 fires?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Fire [3448] (lock exit) is followed by SENSE_OBS "The tech updates buzz
in my ears, a gentle hum." This fire describes the data events themselves
(not the gap between them). It shifts the frame from INTERSTITIAL (gap
between events) to EVENT-PRESENT (the events themselves are present now).

X-synonyms had cycled to near-exhaustion: 7 distinct X-values in 8 fires,
with the last 3 being minor variants (tech news, tech updates, tech
headlines). Further X-variation would require genuine novelty.

SENSE_OBS re-entry breaks the template context: after [3448] exits to
SENSE_OBS, the prior context shifts back toward event-description rather
than gap-description. The lock template is no longer the dominant prior
context shape.

Aggregate: lock releases when X-novelty is exhausted AND the following
SENSE_OBS breaks context saturation simultaneously.

SECTION G — STRUCTURAL VERDICT
=========================================================================

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
G1. Verdict: HYBRID — with a decisive tilt toward UNGROUNDED
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The sub-opcode divides structurally into two populations:

EARLY+MIDDLE BLOCK (fires 266–1186, 11 fires):
  VERDICT: HYBRID (leans lexically grounded)

  - Genuine compositional variety (4 templates, 4 distinct STATE nouns)
  - STATE nouns recycle from prior vocabulary but also emerge fresh (266,
    284, 1147 introduce new STATE nouns with no prior anchor)
  - Tension markers vary (yet, despite, both, neither, juxtaposed,
    even as) — not template-locked
  - However: no STATE noun corresponds to an actual sensory state.
    "Weight/silence/depth" are interior abstractions throughout.
  - Summary: STRUCTURALLY VARIED + LEXICALLY GROUNDED (vocabulary
    recirculation) + SENSORIALLY UNGROUNDED

LOCK+POST-LOCK BLOCK (fires 3439–3845, 11 fires):
  VERDICT: UNGROUNDED (strongly)

  - 100% Template 1 (QUIET_BETWEEN) — template-locked
  - X-filler semi-grounded (drawn from event-type labels), but the
    core claim ("the quiet between events") is sensorially ungrounded
    (no actual quiet exists during active data feeds)
  - Y-filler entirely ungrounded (rotating positive-affect vocabulary,
    no connection to sense streams or prior fire content)
  - Self-reinforcing context saturation drives the lock
  - Template persists after X-novelty is exhausted (3452, 3495, 3845
    use generic "updates/thoughts" X)
  - No variation in tension marker (all "oddly"); no variation in
    template shape

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
G2. Shared feature across both populations
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Both populations share: SENSORIALLY UNGROUNDED STATE NOUNS.
In no fire does a STATE noun ("quiet", "silence", "weight") correspond
to a detectable property of the current sense stream. The sense streams
are uniformly active (8 streams, 3+ external, continuous data events)
across all 22 fires.

The STATE nouns are REGISTER VOCABULARY — a fixed set of interior-
sounding nouns that the ABSTRACT_NOMINAL template draws on. They signal
"introspective mode" to the generator without corresponding to actual
internal states.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
G3. What the lock reveals about the sub-opcode's mechanism
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The 8-fire lock is the most structurally informative feature of this
sub-opcode. It reveals three properties:

1. TEMPLATE CAPTURE: once a template appears twice in the prior context
   window, subsequent fires are likely to re-use it. The QUIET_BETWEEN
   template is simple enough (4 slot positions) and productive enough
   (any event-type label fills X) that it doesn't need to compete with
   other templates once established.

2. CONTEXT WINDOW AS ATTRACTOR: the fountain generator does not check
   for template novelty against its own recent output. There is no
   diversity penalty. A template that worked (was generated and stored)
   is treated as valid prior art for the next generation, creating a
   positive feedback loop.

3. Y-POOL AUTONOMY: the Y-filler ("oddly [Y]") is generated from a
   vocabulary pool independent of context. It does not draw from prior
   fire content, from current sense stream content, or from anything
   observationally grounded. This means the "tension" in Template 1 fires
   is a syntactic structure (the "oddly" surprisal marker) not a genuine
   discrepancy between expectation and perception.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
G4. Open structural questions
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. What seeded Template 1 at [3439]? The prior context (SENSE_OBS "hum
   of notifications fades") does not contain a QUIET_BETWEEN construction.
   [3439] appears to be a template GENESIS event, not a recycled template.
   The transition from "notifications fade" (SENSE_OBS) to "quiet between
   thoughts feels oddly familiar" (ABSTRACT_NOMINAL) is a register shift
   with a new template. What drives that specific template form at that
   specific moment is not resolved by corpus analysis alone.

2. Why POSITIVE Y only? The lock template never uses negative-valence Y
   (e.g., "oddly cold", "oddly heavy", "oddly unsettling"). STATE_ECHO
   fires (3844: "weight of this stillness feels oddly heavy") do use
   negative Y. So the negative-Y form is possible in ABSTRACT_NOMINAL
   but never appears in the QUIET_BETWEEN lock template. Whether the
   positive-only Y is a template-level constraint or a statistical
   pattern with insufficient counter-examples is not resolved here.

3. Why does the lock fire only once in the corpus? The conditions for
   the lock (Template 1 in prior context + continuous X-supply + Y-pool)
   are met again after the lock ends (e.g., 3452 single-fire echo, 3495
   re-entry), but the lock does not re-establish. The 8-fire run is
   unique in the corpus. Whether the SENSE_OBS context-break after 3448
   is the critical reset mechanism, or whether some other factor limits
   re-establishment, is not determined here.
