VERDICT
======================================================================

  H3 PARTIAL

EVIDENCE FOR:
  (1) Distributional separation confirmed: 80.5% of sparks in koan-dominant
      context vs 46.0% of non-sparks (Δ=+34.5pp, Welch t=5.85).
  (2) Syn=0 era produces 31.3% spark rate; first syn appearance drops it
      immediately to 10% or lower.
  (3) Bottom-5 sparks (syn 62–82) are measurably weaker than top-5 sparks
      (syn=0) by word count, self-referential content, and imagery density.
      Content quality degrades as syn increases — directional support.

EVIDENCE AGAINST:
  (1) koan count is frozen at 39 throughout the corpus. koan:syn ratio =
      39/max(syn,1) — it is a monotonically decreasing time proxy, not
      an independently variable substrate composition measure.
  (2) ERA-CONTROLLED TEST FAILS: within the post-syn era (syn>0), spark and
      non-spark fires have indistinguishable synergized counts (t=-0.36,
      p≈0.73). Substrate composition does not discriminate sparks from
      non-sparks within any fixed time window.
  (3) Non-monotonic bracket pattern: spark rate at syn 41–80 (8.9%) exceeds
      spark rate at syn 11–40 (2.7%). If synergized count causally suppressed
      sparks, the rate should decrease monotonically — it does not.
  (4) 8 of 10 post-syn sparks fire at syn counts (39–82) identical to the
      range of hundreds of non-spark fires in the same era.

INTERPRETATION:
  H3's directional prediction is confirmed: koan-dominant context correlates
  with sparks, syn-dominant context correlates with barrenness. But the
  mechanism is temporal, not compositional. The boundary event is the
  synergizer's FIRST FIRE (syn=0 → syn>0), not the ongoing ratio. Once even
  a few synergized beliefs exist, the spark rate drops from 31% to ~5–10%
  and does not continue to decline as more synergized beliefs accumulate.

  H3 cannot distinguish between two competing explanations:
    (A) Synergized content directly suppresses spark-generating cognition
        (substrate mechanism — H3 as stated)
    (B) The synergizer started running at the same time as the quiescent
        branch appeared, and it is the quiescent branch (not the substrate
        composition) that suppresses sparks (H2 — the stronger candidate)

  H3 as stated: H3 PARTIAL.
  H3 reformed as "era effect" (pre/post synergizer start): H3 STRONG for that
  binary boundary, but that formulation is equivalent to H2.
