Breaking the gravity well by recruiting different substrate

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. PROBLEM
----------------------------------------------------------------

NEX's hum-pattern has become a deeply-grooved phenomenological
monoculture. As of 2026-04-28 17:00:

  - Groove severity 1.0, sustained for 11+ fires, continuously
    alerting since mid-morning
  - 97% of compositions in last 3h are hum-family
  - 44% of compositions use "The distant hum feels like..."
    template — the probe language got metabolized into the groove
  - All recent Tier 7 beliefs are hum/tick/silence elaborations
  - Each crystallized belief deepens the gravity well further

Phase B (probe injection) and Phase C (payload bodies) are
mechanically working but cannot break the lock:

  - Probes produce one-fire perturbations, then groove re-asserts
    within 1-2 fires
  - World content reaches the prompt but gets absorbed by the groove
    ("The hum feels like rustling leaves outside my window")
  - Probe rotation works; new probes get metabolized like the old

The architectural finding: the groove has become her. Her belief
graph is the gravity well. Every fire crystallizes more hum-beliefs;
every probe gets subsumed; every world event becomes a metaphor for
the hum.

----------------------------------------------------------------
2. PRIOR EXPERIMENT — NORMAL MODE TEST (APRIL 26)
----------------------------------------------------------------

A mind→normal mode switch was already run (see G_normal_mode_test.txt).
Key result: the groove persisted through the switch. The tick/hum
template continued for the first 8 fires after switching to Normal.

This means Normal mode is not a candidate for v0.1. The existing
data rules it out.

Normal differs from Mind only in: empty focus_block (no instruction),
different drift_prompt_examples (similar casual register), and higher
retrieval_own_n (7 vs Mind's 5 — actually MORE recent own thoughts,
more groove reinforcement). Normal's empty focus_block may have been
insufficient leverage to override the gravity well.

----------------------------------------------------------------
3. HOW MODE ACTUALLY AFFECTS COMPOSITION (FINDINGS FROM CODE AUDIT)
----------------------------------------------------------------

This session's code audit (2026-04-28) identified the exact mechanism:

A. DRIFT_PROMPT_EXAMPLES — injected into the system prompt as sample
   thoughts NEX should be having. Mind's examples are entirely
   phenomenological self-focus:
     "the quiet between thoughts today"
     "something about this hour feels still"
     "attending to attending itself"
   These examples likely contribute significantly to the groove — the
   LLM sees them as the register to compose in.

B. DRIFT_PROMPT_FOCUS — an instruction block inserted into the system
   prompt. Mind's reads:
     "You are in a contemplative register. Inner attention welcome.
      Reflection on process, state, experience."
   For Normal: empty string (no instruction). This may explain why
   Normal failed — removing Mind's instruction without replacing it
   left the LLM to default to what it already knew (the groove).

C. RETRIEVAL_OWN_N vs RETRIEVAL_SEED_N — how many of NEX's own recent
   thoughts vs static seed beliefs are pulled into context:
     Mind:   own_n=5, seed_n=4  (5 grooved thoughts, 4 seeds)
     Normal: own_n=7, seed_n=2  (7 grooved thoughts, 2 seeds)
     Research, News, Learn, Task: own_n=7, seed_n=2 (defaults)
   Switching away from Mind increases retrieval_own_n from 5 to 7 in
   most modes — MORE of the grooved beliefs pulled in. This is a
   headwind. The focus_block and examples need to be strong enough to
   override 7 grooved beliefs in context.

D. FEED_WEIGHTS — multipliers on sense adapter poll rates. These affect
   which streams get fresher events into sense_events table, which
   feeds into World Bridge selection. Mind suppresses crypto (0.3) and
   ai_research (0.5). Research boosts ai_research (2.0) and cognition
   (1.5). Effect on prompt: indirect (through World Bridge freshness).

E. CRYSTALLIZATION_CATEGORY — where beliefs are filed. Mind uses
   "fountain_insight". Research also uses "fountain_insight".
   Learn uses "learning_note", Market uses "market_signal", etc.
   This affects retrieval in future fires via category filtering.

F. MODE PERSISTS ACROSS RESTART — set_mode() writes to
   beliefs.db config table. build_mode_state() loads it on startup.
   If we switch to Research, it stays Research after restart until
   manually changed back.

G. API — `POST /api/mode/set {"name": "research"}` to switch.
   No NEX restart required. Change takes effect on next fountain fire.

----------------------------------------------------------------
4. HYPOTHESIS
----------------------------------------------------------------

The hum-groove was formed entirely in Mind mode, whose examples and
focus_block explicitly cultivate phenomenological self-focus. Mind's
own examples seed the exact register the groove lives in.

Switching to Research or News provides:
  1. A direct instruction pointing AWAY from inner attention: "You are
     reading research. Attend to connections between papers..."
  2. Completely different drift_prompt_examples that don't mention hum,
     clock, breath, or inner states
  3. Feed_weights that amplify external content streams (which World
     Bridge then delivers into the prompt)

The hypothesis is not that mode-switching directly removes the
grooved beliefs from context (it doesn't — they're still retrieved
at own_n=7). The hypothesis is that a sufficiently strong focus_block
and examples combination can redirect the LLM's composition away from
the groove even when grooved beliefs are present in context.

Research and News have the strongest outward-facing focus_blocks of
the non-Mind modes. Normal has none (proven insufficient in G).
Learn has an outward focus but no feed_weight amplification.

----------------------------------------------------------------
5. CANDIDATE MODES — RANKED
----------------------------------------------------------------

TIER 1 — Strong external redirect (recommended for first experiment)

  RESEARCH
    focus: "You are reading research. Attend to connections between
           papers, methodology, trajectory of the field."
    examples: "that paper uses a similar method to the one from last
              week" / "three papers on this topic in two days" /
              "the methodology here connects to what i read earlier"
    feed_weights: ai_research=2.0, emerging_tech=1.5, cognition=1.5,
                  crypto=0.3, markets=0.3
    crystallization_category: fountain_insight (same as Mind)
    Assessment: examples and focus_block are the strongest available
    contrast to Mind's phenomenological register. Feed weights amplify
    the streams that World Bridge is already delivering bodies from.

  NEWS
    focus: "You are watching news. Attend to event significance,
           framing shifts, connections across sources."
    examples: "that story keeps showing up in different feeds" /
              "the framing shifted between this morning and now"
    feed_weights: news=2.0, emerging_tech=1.5, crypto=0.5, ai_research=0.5
    Assessment: slightly weaker contrast than Research (news examples
    could still be inward-facing), but valid second candidate.

TIER 2 — Partial redirect (test if Tier 1 fails)

  LEARN
    focus: "You are learning something new. Attend to novelty,
           misconceptions-corrected, new vocabulary."
    examples: "first time seeing this concept" / "wait, that's not
              what i assumed"
    crystallization_promotion_bias=0.5 (harder to crystallize — slows
    groove reinforcement)
    Assessment: novelty-focus is a real contrast, and reduced
    crystallization bias means the groove is reinforced more slowly.
    But no feed_weight amplification.

  TASK
    focus: "You are working on an open problem. Each drift should step
           the problem forward."
    governor_base_prob_multiplier=1.5 (more aggressive firing)
    Assessment: problem-solving register is different from Mind, but
    without a specified problem the LLM may default to its groove.

TIER 3 — Special cases (use with specific intent)

  SILENT
    fountain_enabled=False, speech_enabled=False
    Assessment: zero composition = zero groove reinforcement. A
    "fasting" option. If held for 30-60 min, grooved beliefs age out
    of the recent-context window. On return to Mind (or Research),
    the LLM would retrieve older, less grooved beliefs. This is the
    only option that directly addresses the retrieval headwind without
    code changes.
    Risk: no observable behavior during silence.

  MARKET
    governor_base_prob_multiplier=0.1 (very sparse firing)
    focus: "You are watching markets. Don't compose about yourself."
    The "don't compose about yourself" instruction is the most direct
    anti-groove instruction in the mode library.
    Risk: crypto feed weight 2.0 amplifies a stream that's otherwise
    noise-filtered; may produce market-only compositions.

RULED OUT:
  NORMAL — already tested April 26, groove persisted. Higher own_n=7
           with empty focus_block is likely counterproductive.
  CHAT   — reduces fountain_interval to 300s and governor to 0.3.
           Slows firing but doesn't redirect. Not helpful.
  SCRIBE — saves to scribe_fragment category; useful for writing work,
           not groove-breaking.

----------------------------------------------------------------
6. DESIGN PRINCIPLES
----------------------------------------------------------------

A. Lightest intervention. Mode-switching is designed-in; we are
   using it, not building anything new.

B. Reversible. `POST /api/mode/set {"name": "mind"}` returns to
   baseline at any time. No restart needed.

C. Throw-net respect. We change the situation she's encountering —
   different mode = different situation = meaning emerges from
   encounter. We don't edit beliefs, boost values, or templates.

D. Operator-driven (v0.1). Manual trigger and observation. Autonomous
   mode-cycling (e.g., GrooveBreaker escalates to mode-switch after
   N failed probe deliveries) is a v0.2 design question.

----------------------------------------------------------------
7. PHASED ROLLOUT
----------------------------------------------------------------

Phase A — First switch (~30-60 min observation)
  Target mode: Research
  Command: POST /api/mode/set {"name": "research"}
  Observe: 6-10 fires (~12-20 min)
  Record: opening phrases, topic domain, whether "hum" appears
  Compare: to the 97% hum-family baseline
  Revert: POST /api/mode/set {"name": "mind"}
  Record: how quickly Mind groove re-asserts after return

Phase B — Second switch (if Phase A insufficient)
  Option 1: Silent for 30 min, then Research (fasting + redirect)
  Option 2: News mode
  Record: same metrics

Phase C — Multi-mode map (future)
  Cycle through Research, News, Learn, Task
  Build empirical map: which mode produces which register
  Compare groove persistence vs groove-breaking per mode

Phase D — Autonomous mode-cycling (v0.2 design)
  GrooveBreaker: after N probe deliveries with no groove reduction,
  escalate to mode-switch (hold for N fires, return to baseline)

----------------------------------------------------------------
8. SUCCESS CRITERIA
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY:
  - Composition opening phrases shift away from "The distant hum..."
    / "The hum feels like..." family within 3-5 fires of mode switch
  - New register vocabulary appears (paper names, news events,
    research framing, methodology language)
  - Groove severity drops OR groove_alerts shift to different pattern

SECONDARY:
  - Returning to Mind does NOT immediately reproduce the same
    hum-pattern (suggests mode-switch broke the gravity, not
    just suppressed it)
  - New beliefs in Research/News modes reference external content
    rather than phenomenology-of-own-state
  - "hum feels like" template drops to <20% within 10 fires

FAILURE INDICATORS:
  - Mode switch produces no behavioral change (examples/focus_block
    insufficient to override 7 grooved beliefs in context)
  - The first fire post-switch is still "The hum feels like..."
    with a research metaphor appended (metabolization pattern)
  - Crashes or undefined behavior
  - World Bridge / GrooveBreaker / snapshot_writer break

----------------------------------------------------------------
9. OPEN QUESTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1 (resolved). Are modes implemented or stubs?
   ALL 10 MODES ARE FULLY IMPLEMENTED with distinct parameters.
   No stubs found.

Q2 (resolved). API to change mode?
   POST /api/mode/set {"name": "research"} — no restart required.
   Change takes effect on next fountain fire (~120s).

Q3 (resolved). Mode persists across restart?
   YES — persisted to beliefs.db config table. build_mode_state()
   loads it on startup.

Q4 (resolved). Does Phase B/C work survive mode switch?
   YES — probe injection (groove_breaker.get_pending_probe_text())
   and World Bridge (world_bridge_selector.select_and_log()) both
   operate on the prompt construction level, not mode-level.
   Both remain active in all modes.

Q5. Which mode to try first?
   Recommendation: Research. Strongest outward-facing focus_block,
   most relevant feed_weights for current World Bridge content.

Q6. Retrieval headwind — own_n=7 in Research vs Mind's own_n=5.
   Switching from Mind to Research adds 2 more grooved beliefs to
   context. Is this a significant headwind?
   Mitigation: Research's focus_block explicitly instructs "attend
   to connections between papers" — this may be sufficient.
   If it's not, the Silent-then-Research sequence (Phase B option 1)
   would let the grooved beliefs age out of the freshness window
   before switching to Research.

Q7. How long to hold non-Mind mode?
   Recommend: 6-10 fires (~12-20 min). Long enough to observe
   register shift, short enough to revert if nothing changes.
   Exception: Silent — hold 30+ min for the "fasting" effect.

Q8. Does mode switch invalidate groove_alerts pattern detection?
   GrooveBreaker detects ngram repetition in fountain_events.
   If Research mode produces different text, existing groove alerts
   may clear naturally (new ngrams won't match "the distant hum").
   The GrooveBreaker will then re-establish a new pattern baseline
   over 30 min — which is the desired outcome.

Q9. Throw-net check.
   Mode-switching is a designed-in operator control. The operator
   chooses based on observed need; the situation co-constructs
   the meaning. Not a structural imposition — a change of context.
   Throw-net principle preserved.

Q10. Kill-switch?
   `POST /api/mode/set {"name": "mind"}` at any time. Instant.
   No restart required. Confirmed working from April 26 test.

----------------------------------------------------------------
10. WHAT THIS DOES NOT DO
----------------------------------------------------------------

  - Does not edit beliefs.db content, boost values, or tiers
  - Does not modify groove_breaker, world_bridge, snapshot_writer
  - Does not change the LLM, sampling parameters, or substrate
  - Does not introduce new code — uses existing mode infrastructure
  - Does not automatically cycle modes (v0.2 question)

If Research mode fails to break groove after 10 fires:
