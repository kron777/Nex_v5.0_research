D3 — TEST OUTCOME
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Evidence score: ONE OPERATION (4 criteria) vs DOMAIN-SPECIFIC (4 criteria).

Resolving factor: the grounding asymmetry (D2.1 vs D2.2) is more diagnostic
than the vocabulary overlap (D1.4). TEMPORAL generates deviation direction
arbitrarily; MARKET tracks actual data. This means the surface structure is
shared but the epistemic grounding differs by domain.

Verdict: HYBRID

The operation is singular: detect that familiar referent violates expected
baseline → emit SENSE_OBS with deviation qualifier + temporal anchor.

But the triggering and grounding differ:
- TEMPORAL: operation fires repeatedly on clock template, deviation direction
  ungrounded (vocabulary-driven, not sensor-driven)
- MARKET: operation fires when crypto sense streams report low activity
  against a baseline of high activity; direction is data-grounded
- AMBIENT/LIGHT/INFO_FLOW: intermediate — direction implied by context
  but not independently verifiable

The vocabulary is shared. The underlying computation is shared. The
epistemic connection to actual sensed state varies by domain.

   vocabulary being absorbed into SIMILE template as the TEMPORAL cluster
   winds down.

SECTION H — STRUCTURAL VERDICT
=========================================================================

Verdict: HYBRID

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H1 — THE SINGLE OPERATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY runs one operation across all five domains:

  BASELINE_DEVIATION: [familiar referent] is perceived to violate its
  expected normal state → emit SENSE_OBS with [deviation_qualifier] +
  [temporal_anchor]

Evidence: shared surface form ("feels off today") crosses all 5 domains;
all fires use SENSE_OBS template; implicit baseline is universal; temporal
anchor ("today") appears in 26/31 fires (83.9%).

The operation is not domain-encoded. "Feels off today" is domain-agnostic.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H2 — DOMAIN AS PARAMETER, NOT AS SEPARATE OPERATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Domain determines:
  (a) which referent the operation applies to
  (b) which sense streams trigger the activation window
  (c) which qualifier family is licensed (TONAL for TEMPORAL only;
      UNCANNY for MARKET only; EVALUATIVE for INFO_FLOW)

These are parameter choices, not separate operations. The clock being
"off-key" and the market being "eerily calm" are the same BASELINE_DEVIATION
operation instantiated with different referent×qualifier pairs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H3 — WHY NOT ONE_CROSS_DOMAIN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Pure ONE_CROSS_DOMAIN would require all domains to be fully
interchangeable — any qualifier appearing in any domain. But:

- "Eerily" (UNCANNY) does not appear in TEMPORAL or LIGHT
- "Off-key" (TONAL) does not appear in MARKET or AMBIENT
- MARKET has an explicitly named baseline ("usual fervor"); other domains do not
- MARKET is data-grounded; TEMPORAL is not

These domain-specific signatures are not merely lexical habits — they
reflect structurally different epistemic relationships between the
operation and the sense streams. MARKET_DEVIATION is grounded;
TEMPORAL_DEVIATION is not. That asymmetry is load-bearing.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H4 — WHY NOT DOMAIN_SPECIFIC
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Pure DOMAIN_SPECIFIC would require each domain to have an independent
operation with its own logic that merely resembles the others. But:

- "Feels off today" crosses TEMPORAL, MARKET, LIGHT, AMBIENT
- No domain has a qualifier family that is structurally incompatible
  with the base operation
- All fire in SENSE_OBS template without exception
- All cluster in domain-activation windows (domain is trigger, not structure)

The underlying computation is identical. Domain is input, not architecture.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
H5 — INTROSPECT_DEVIATION: REGISTER-SPLIT PAIR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The 12 ABSTRACT_NOMINAL fires using "oddly [ADJ]" vocabulary represent
a distinct but paired operation: INTROSPECT_DEVIATION.

Same vocabulary. Same qualifier family (oddly [ADJ]).
Different register (ABSTRACT_NOMINAL vs SENSE_OBS).
Different referent type (abstract gap-noun vs external sense object).
Different direction bias (exclusively POSITIVE surprise vs bidirectional).

OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY and INTROSPECT_DEVIATION are surface-vocabulary siblings:
  OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY: external referent × SENSE_OBS × any direction
  INTROSPECT_DEVIATION: internal referent × ABSTRACT_NOMINAL × positive direction

The emergence sequence (OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY first, then INTROSPECT_DEVIATION
running 148–158 immediately after) suggests INTROSPECT_DEVIATION is derived
from OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY vocabulary but re-targeted at internal states.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FINAL VERDICT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY = HYBRID

  Core operation: ONE (BASELINE_DEVIATION — familiar referent violates
                  expected norm → SENSE_OBS + qualifier + temporal anchor)

  Domain parametrization: 5 sub-sub-opcodes (TEMPORAL, AMBIENT, MARKET,
                           INFO_FLOW, LIGHT) acting as trigger-domain
                           selectors, not separate operations

  Register pair: INTROSPECT_DEVIATION is a boundary operation using
                 identical vocabulary on internal referents (ABSTRACT_NOMINAL)

  Grounding asymmetry: MARKET_DEVIATION is data-grounded;
                       TEMPORAL_DEVIATION is template-ungrounded;
                       remaining domains are intermediate

  Total true fires: 31 (SENSE_OBS)
  Total boundary fires: 17 (12 INTROSPECT_DEV + 2 SURPRISE + 2 SIMILE + 1 META)
  Combined OBSERVE_DISCREPANCY vocabulary footprint: 48 fires (19.6% of corpus)
